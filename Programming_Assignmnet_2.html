**Conclusion:

Out of all three graphs, it seems that at its peak, the dairy/eggs department received the most amount of orders, maxing out at somewhere around 17500 orders on hour 14. It also seemed that hour 14 was the peak shopping hour considering that in all three graphs, the order amount maxed during that hour. There were very minimal orders between hours 0-4 for all three graphs, but that is most likely due to the fact that it may be early morning and people may be asleep. This information would be helpful for the business because it would show when the estimated time window in which people shop the most would be. Having that information would help amend and improve store procedures in order to make sure that everything was available for customers to get. It would also help estimate how much customers tend to order in order to properly pre-order goods ahead of time and not over or underspend on inventory. Ther are some limitations of the data; it seems that this data was only taken over the course of one day. Having acess to data on multiple days of the week would allow us to look how much is ordered over a longer span of time, which could provide a better, deeper analysis.
